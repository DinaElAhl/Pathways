#The E² Teaching Framework
Featured framework
E² — "Every Student Has the Right to Understand" — is an original teaching framework by Dina El Ahl. It is the pedagogical spine behind every suggested path and flipped-video slot on Pathways. Open the official E² app →

Core beliefs
Every student has the right to understand.
Teacher adaptation comes first — not student blame.
Struggle signals a need for adjustment, not inability.
No student is unteachable.
Teachers modify materials and methods to meet learners.
Understanding is a fundamental right.
The four pillars
#Pillar 1 — Lesson Design (5-part blueprint)
Every strong lesson moves through five phases:

Hook (2 min) — attention-grabbing opening that makes the lesson feel worth showing up for.
Teach (10–15 min) — chunked content delivery; one idea at a time, with worked examples.
Practice (15–20 min) — guided then independent work; students do the cognitive lifting.
Connect (5 min) — real-world relevance; answer "why does this matter to me?"
Reflect (5 min) — exit ticket that surfaces what was learned and what still isn't clear.
#Pillar 2 — Differentiation
Reach every learner, not just the average one:

Modalities — auditory, visual, and kinesthetic entry points for every new concept.
Tiered tasks — struggling, on-level, and advanced versions of the same core task.
Cultural responsiveness — examples, names, and contexts that reflect every learner in the room.
Student choice — genuine options in how students show what they know.
#Pillar 3 — Engagement (6 levers)
Six deliberate levers you can pull to lift engagement:

Curiosity — open with a question that begs an answer.
Active participation — no passive watching; every student is doing something.
Purpose — name why today's lesson matters beyond the test.
Feedback — fast, specific, actionable — not just grades.
Gamification — points, quests, or teams where it genuinely helps (not as decoration).
Routine & novelty balance — predictable structure, surprising moments inside it.
#Pillar 4 — Classroom Culture
None of the above lands if the room isn't safe to try in:

Relationship-building — know your students as people first.
Co-created norms — students shape the rules, so they own them.
Restorative practices — repair harm; don't just punish.
Welcoming environment — physical space, language, and tone all say "you belong here."

#Interoperability
LTI 1.3 (Learning Tools Interoperability) — the modern OAuth2 / OpenID-Connect-based spec for launching tools from an LMS (Canvas, Moodle, Blackboard, Brightspace, Google Classroom). Supports Deep Linking, Assignment & Grade Services (AGS), and Names & Role Provisioning Services (NRPS). Maintained by 1EdTech.
xAPI (Experience API, formerly Tin Can) — records learning as actor → verb → object statements in a Learning Record Store (LRS). Works across devices, offline, and outside an LMS.
SCORM 1.2 / 2004 — the legacy packaging standard for self-contained e-learning courses. Use xAPI for new work when possible.
cmi5 — the xAPI profile designed to replace SCORM for LMS-delivered content.
QTI (Question & Test Interoperability) — a spec for exchanging assessment items and tests between platforms. QuizForge and RubricRaft import/export QTI 2.2 and 3.0.
OneRoster — rostering data (classes, students, enrolments) between an SIS and learning platforms.
Caliper Analytics — 1EdTech's alternative to xAPI with a stricter profile model.
#Pedagogy (beyond E²)
UDL (Universal Design for Learning) 3.0 — design instruction with multiple means of engagement, representation, and action & expression. Maintained by CAST.
Bloom's Taxonomy (revised) — six cognitive levels (remember, understand, apply, analyze, evaluate, create).
SAMR — evaluate tech use: Substitution, Augmentation, Modification, Redefinition.
TPACK — the intersection of Technological, Pedagogical, and Content Knowledge.
5E Instructional Model — Engage, Explore, Explain, Elaborate, Evaluate. Common in science teaching.
ADDIE — instructional design cycle: Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation, Evaluation.
Constructive Alignment (Biggs) — align intended outcomes, teaching, and assessment.
Montessori · Waldorf · Reggio Emilia — long-established alternative pedagogies with child-led, inquiry-driven traditions.
Project-Based Learning (PBL) — extended, authentic projects as the core unit of instruction.
#Compliance & privacy
FERPA (US) — protects the privacy of student education records at institutions receiving US federal funding.
COPPA (US) — restricts collection of personal information from children under 13.
GDPR & GDPR-K (EU) — baseline data-protection plus stricter rules for children (age threshold varies 13–16 by member state).
PIPEDA (Canada), APPI (Japan), POPIA (South Africa), LGPD (Brazil) — regional equivalents.
PDPL (Saudi Arabia), UAE PDPL, Egyptian PDPL — personal-data-protection laws across the Arab world.
SOC 2 Type II — audit framework; common procurement requirement.
ISO 27001 — information security management.
#Accessibility
WCAG 2.2 (AA) — baseline for accessible web content.
Section 508 (US) — federal procurement rule referencing WCAG.
EN 301 549 (EU) — European accessibility standard for ICT procurement.
ARIA Authoring Practices — WAI guidance on accessible rich-internet-application patterns.

#Arabic language & Quran studies
Pathways gives first-class treatment to Arabic-as-a-second-language and Quran studies — with dedicated apps, resources, and path templates.

Arabic for non-native speakers
CEFR mapping (A1–C2) — all non-native Arabic resources are tagged to CEFR levels.
Al-Arabiyya Bayna Yadayk — the most widely used series for Arabic as a foreign language.
Madinah Arabic Reader — the classic grammar-through-reading approach.
ALPS / ACTFL-OPI — oral proficiency frameworks applicable to Arabic learners.
Diglossia-aware instruction — when to teach Modern Standard Arabic (Fusha) vs. dialect (Levantine, Egyptian, Gulf, Maghrebi).
Quran studies

Mutashabihat — similar-verse hints for memorizers, surfaced by the HifzHelper app.

Where to start
If you're teaching or learning: E² + UDL 3.0 + CEFR (for languages) covers most of the "how do I reach every learner?" question. If you're building: LTI 1.3 + xAPI + WCAG 2.2 AA covers "will this plug in and be usable?" Layer in regional compliance (FERPA, GDPR-K, PDPL) as your context requires.
