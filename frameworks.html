⭐ E² Teaching Framework Featured
E² — "Every Student Has the Right to Understand" — is an original teaching framework by Dina El Ahl. It is the pedagogical spine behind every suggested path and flipped-video slot on Pathways.
Original Framework
🎓
E² — Every Student Has the Right to Understand
E² rests on six core beliefs: every student has the right to understand; teacher adaptation comes first — not student blame; struggle signals a need for adjustment, not inability; no student is unteachable; teachers modify materials and methods to meet learners; understanding is a fundamental right.
Pillar 1
📋 Lesson Design
5-part blueprint: Hook (2 min) → Teach (10–15 min) → Practice (15–20 min) → Connect (5 min) → Reflect (5 min).
Pillar 2
🎯 Differentiation
Modalities, tiered tasks, cultural responsiveness, and student choice to reach every learner.
Pillar 3
⚡ Engagement
Six levers: curiosity, active participation, purpose, feedback, gamification, routine & novelty balance.
Pillar 4
🏫 Classroom Culture
Relationship-building, co-created norms, restorative practices, welcoming environment.
Where in Pathways: FlipStudio auto-generates the 5-part blueprint. LessonLoom and RubricRaft use E² as their default template. Path Builder surfaces E² first when no other framework is selected.
🔗 Interoperability Standards
Standards that let Pathways plug into your LMS and learning record store without vendor lock-in.
Interop
LTI 1.3
The modern OAuth2/OpenID-Connect spec for launching tools from an LMS (Canvas, Moodle, Blackboard, Brightspace, Google Classroom). Supports Deep Linking, AGS, and NRPS.
Pathways: All apps launch via LTI 1.3 with grade passback.
Interop
xAPI (Tin Can)
Records learning as actor→verb→object statements in an LRS. Works across devices, offline, and outside an LMS. Preferred for new work over SCORM.
Pathways: QuizForge and ExitTicket emit xAPI statements to any conformant LRS.
Interop
SCORM 1.2 / 2004
Legacy packaging standard for self-contained e-learning courses. Use xAPI for new builds; SCORM export is provided for legacy platforms.
Pathways: SCORM export available for compatibility.
Interop
QTI 2.2 & 3.0
Question and Test Interoperability — the spec for exchanging assessment items between platforms. Enables import from and export to other assessment tools.
Pathways: QuizForge and RubricRaft import/export QTI 2.2 and 3.0.
Interop
OneRoster
Rostering data (classes, students, enrolments) between an SIS and learning platforms. Reduces admin burden and keeps class lists in sync.
Pathways: DataDash syncs rosters via OneRoster 1.2.
Interop
cmi5
The xAPI profile designed to replace SCORM for LMS-delivered content. Adds launch, session management, and completion handling on top of xAPI.
Pathways: cmi5 launch profile supported for LMS packages.
📚 Pedagogy
Established instructional-design and teaching frameworks that Pathways aligns to and maps into its tools.
Pedagogy
UDL 3.0
Universal Design for Learning — design instruction with multiple means of engagement, representation, and action & expression. Maintained by CAST.
Pathways: DifferentiateIt generates UDL-compliant multi-modal task variants.
Pedagogy
Bloom's Taxonomy (Revised)
Six cognitive levels: Remember, Understand, Apply, Analyse, Evaluate, Create. Used to level learning objectives and assessment items.
Pathways: LessonLoom tags every objective and QuizForge aligns questions to Bloom's levels.
Pedagogy
5E Instructional Model
Engage, Explore, Explain, Elaborate, Evaluate. Common in science teaching; each phase has a distinct purpose and student action.
Pathways: Available as a path template in the Builder.
Pedagogy
SAMR
Substitution, Augmentation, Modification, Redefinition — a model for evaluating technology use in the classroom. Helps avoid "tech for tech's sake".
Pathways: Each app is tagged with its SAMR level.
Pedagogy
Project-Based Learning (PBL)
Extended, authentic projects as the core unit of instruction. Backed by PBL Works Gold Standard: driving question, student voice, reflection, and public product.
Pathways: ProjectPilot is built around PBL Works standards.
Pedagogy
ADDIE
Instructional design cycle: Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation, Evaluation. The backbone of formal course design.
Pathways: Path Builder wizard mirrors ADDIE stages.
🔒 Compliance & Privacy
Legal and regulatory frameworks that govern student data. Pathways is designed with privacy-by-default.
Compliance
FERPA (US)
Protects the privacy of student education records at institutions receiving US federal funding.
Compliance
GDPR & GDPR-K (EU)
Baseline data protection plus stricter rules for children. Age threshold for consent varies 13–16 by member state.
Compliance
COPPA (US)
Restricts collection of personal information from children under 13 for US-facing services.
Compliance
PDPL (Saudi Arabia)
Saudi Arabia's Personal Data Protection Law — covers personal data collected or processed in the Kingdom.
Compliance
UAE PDPL & Egyptian PDPL
Personal data protection laws across the UAE and Egypt. Pathways applies the strictest applicable standard when operating across multiple Arab jurisdictions.
Compliance
LGPD (Brazil) & PIPEDA (Canada)
Regional data-protection equivalents for Latin America and Canada. Pathways maps privacy controls to these laws for schools in those jurisdictions.
♿ Accessibility
Standards that ensure every learner, regardless of ability, can access Pathways tools.
Accessibility
WCAG 2.2 (AA)
Web Content Accessibility Guidelines — the baseline for accessible web content. All Pathways apps target AA conformance.
Accessibility
Section 508 (US)
US federal procurement rule referencing WCAG. Required for schools and districts using federal funds.
Accessibility
EN 301 549 (EU)
European accessibility standard for ICT procurement. Maps closely to WCAG 2.1 with additional mobile and software requirements.
Accessibility
ARIA Authoring Practices
WAI guidance on accessible rich-internet-application patterns. Used throughout the Pathways component library.

📿 Arabic & Quran Studies
Pathways gives first-class treatment to Arabic-as-a-second-language and Quran studies — with dedicated apps, resources, and path templates.
First-class
CEFR Mapping for Arabic (A1–C2)
All non-native Arabic resources are tagged to CEFR levels. ArabicLab and VocabVault surface content appropriate to the learner's level.
First-class
Tajweed & Hifz Standards
Hafs 'an 'Asim is the default qira'ah on Pathways. HifzHelper includes mutashabihat alerts, murajaah cycle planning, and ijazah-holder tagging.
First-class
Islamic Studies Scope
Fiqh (four Sunni madhahib + Ja'fari), Seerah, Aqeedah, Hadith sciences, Adab & Akhlaq — all level-tagged for K-12 through adult learners.
First-class
Diglossia-Aware Instruction
Pathways distinguishes Modern Standard Arabic (Fusha) from regional dialects (Levantine, Egyptian, Gulf, Maghrebi) and supports switching between them in lesson plans.
