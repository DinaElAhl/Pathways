Featured framework

E² Teaching Framework

An original framework by Dina El Ahl — the pedagogical spine behind every Pathways path, app, and template.

Original Framework
E² — Every Student Has the Right to Understand
Six core beliefs: every student has the right to understand; teacher adaptation comes first — not student blame; struggle signals a need for adjustment, not inability; no student is unteachable; teachers modify materials and methods to meet learners; understanding is a fundamental right.
Pillar 1

📋 Lesson Design

5-part blueprint: Hook (2 min) → Teach (10–15 min) → Practice (15–20 min) → Connect (5 min) → Reflect (5 min).

Pillar 2

🎯 Differentiation

Modalities, tiered tasks, cultural responsiveness, and student choice to reach every learner.

Pillar 3

⚡ Engagement

Six levers: curiosity, active participation, purpose, feedback, gamification, routine & novelty balance.

Pillar 4

🏫 Classroom Culture

Relationship-building, co-created norms, restorative practices, welcoming environment.

In Pathways: FlipStudio auto-generates the 5-part blueprint. LessonLoom and RubricRaft use E² as their default template. Path Builder surfaces E² first when no other framework is selected.

Interoperability

Standards that connect Pathways to your LMS

Plug Pathways apps into Canvas, Moodle, Blackboard, Brightspace and Google Classroom without vendor lock-in.

Interop
LTI 1.3
Modern OAuth2/OpenID-Connect spec for launching tools from an LMS. Supports Deep Linking, AGS grade passback, and NRPS roster sync.
Pathways: All apps launch via LTI 1.3 with automatic grade passback.
Interop
xAPI (Tin Can)
Records learning as actor→verb→object statements in an LRS. Works across devices, offline, and outside an LMS. Preferred over SCORM for new work.
Pathways: QuizForge and ExitTicket emit xAPI statements to any conformant LRS.
Interop
SCORM 1.2 / 2004
Legacy packaging standard for self-contained e-learning courses. Use xAPI for new builds; SCORM export is provided for legacy platforms.
Pathways: SCORM export available for compatibility with older LMSes.
Interop
QTI 2.2 & 3.0
Question and Test Interoperability — exchange assessment items between platforms. Import from and export to other assessment tools.
Pathways: QuizForge and RubricRaft import/export QTI 2.2 and 3.0.
Interop
OneRoster 1.2
Syncs rostering data (classes, students, enrolments) between an SIS and learning platforms. Reduces admin burden and keeps class lists in sync.
Pathways: DataDash syncs rosters via OneRoster 1.2.
Interop
cmi5
The xAPI profile designed to replace SCORM for LMS-delivered content. Adds launch, session, and completion management on top of xAPI.
Pathways: cmi5 launch profile supported for LMS packages.

Pedagogy

Instructional frameworks Pathways aligns to

Established teaching and design frameworks mapped into Pathways tools and path templates.

Pedagogy
UDL 3.0
Universal Design for Learning — multiple means of engagement, representation, and action & expression. Maintained by CAST.
Pathways: DifferentiateIt generates UDL-compliant multi-modal task variants.
Pedagogy
Bloom’s Taxonomy (Revised)
Six cognitive levels: Remember, Understand, Apply, Analyse, Evaluate, Create. Used to level objectives and assessment items.
Pathways: LessonLoom tags objectives; QuizForge aligns questions to Bloom’s levels.
Pedagogy
5E Instructional Model
Engage, Explore, Explain, Elaborate, Evaluate. Common in science teaching; each phase has a distinct purpose and student action.
Pathways: Available as a path template in Path Builder.
Pedagogy
SAMR
Substitution, Augmentation, Modification, Redefinition — a model for evaluating technology use. Helps avoid “tech for tech’s sake”.
Pathways: Each app is tagged with its SAMR level.
Pedagogy
Project-Based Learning
Extended, authentic projects as the core unit of instruction. Backed by PBL Works Gold Standard: driving question, student voice, reflection, public product.
Pathways: ProjectPilot is built around PBL Works standards.
Pedagogy
ADDIE
Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation, Evaluation. The backbone of formal course and curriculum design.
Pathways: Path Builder wizard mirrors the ADDIE stages.

Compliance & Privacy

Student data regulations

Pathways is designed privacy-by-default and maps controls to the laws applicable in your jurisdiction.

Compliance
FERPA (US)
Protects the privacy of student education records at institutions receiving US federal funding. Required for all US K–12 and higher ed tools.
Compliance
GDPR & GDPR-K (EU)
Baseline data protection plus stricter rules for children. Age threshold for consent varies 13–16 by member state.
Compliance
COPPA (US)
Restricts collection of personal information from children under 13 for US-facing services.
Compliance
PDPL (Saudi Arabia)
Saudi Arabia’s Personal Data Protection Law — covers personal data collected or processed in the Kingdom.
Compliance
UAE PDPL & Egyptian PDPL
Personal data protection laws across the UAE and Egypt. Pathways applies the strictest applicable standard when operating across multiple Arab jurisdictions.
Compliance
LGPD, PIPEDA, POPIA
Regional data-protection laws for Brazil, Canada, and South Africa. Pathways maps privacy controls to each for schools in those jurisdictions.

Accessibility

Every learner, regardless of ability

Pathways apps target WCAG 2.2 AA conformance and comply with international accessibility procurement standards.

Accessibility
WCAG 2.2 (AA)
Web Content Accessibility Guidelines — the baseline for accessible web content. All Pathways apps target AA conformance.
Accessibility
Section 508 (US)
US federal procurement rule referencing WCAG. Required for schools and districts using federal funds.
Accessibility
EN 301 549 (EU)
European accessibility standard for ICT procurement. Maps closely to WCAG 2.1 with additional mobile and software requirements.
Accessibility
ARIA Authoring Practices
WAI guidance on accessible rich-internet-application patterns. Used throughout the Pathways component library.

Arabic & Quran

First-class Arabic & Islamic studies

Dedicated apps, resources, and path templates for Arabic-as-a-second-language and Quran studies.

First-class
CEFR Mapping for Arabic (A1–C2)
All non-native Arabic resources tagged to CEFR levels. ArabicLab and VocabVault surface content appropriate to the learner’s level. Distinguishes Fusha from dialect skills.
First-class
Tajweed & Hifz Standards
Hafs ‘an ‘Asim is the default qira’ah. HifzHelper includes mutashabihat alerts, murajaah cycle planning, and ijazah-holder tagging for tutors.
First-class
Islamic Studies Scope
Fiqh (four Sunni madhahib + Ja’fari), Seerah, Aqeedah, Hadith sciences, Adab & Akhlaq — all level-tagged for K-12 through adult learners.
First-class
Diglossia-Aware Instruction
Pathways distinguishes Modern Standard Arabic (Fusha) from dialects (Levantine, Egyptian, Gulf, Maghrebi) and supports switching in lesson plans.
